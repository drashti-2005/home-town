Economy of Kumbhan

Agriculture

Farming is the backbone of Kumbhan's economy. The fertile land and favorable climate allow for crops like onion, groundnut, and cotton. Onion farming is especially significant during the Rabi season.

  • Onion – Most profitable crop
  • Groundnut – Commonly grown during Kharif
  • Cotton – Grown occasionally due to price fluctuations

Local Businesses

A number of families run grocery shops, hardware stores, tea stalls, and small trade services. These shops play a vital role in the village’s self-sustainability and support daily needs.

  • Grocery and retail stores
  • Tea stalls and food vendors
  • Mobile repair and hardware shops

Diamond Industry

A portion of the younger population commutes to Mahuva and Surat to work in diamond polishing factories. This sector offers stable employment, especially for youth without farmland.

  • Diamond cutting and polishing jobs
  • Mostly male workers
  • Weekly commute or seasonal migration

Other Occupations

Besides agriculture and trade, villagers also earn through daily wage labor, transportation services, tailoring, and home-based craft work. Some women make papad or pickles for extra income.

  • Daily wage labor
  • Transportation (tractor, auto-rickshaw)
  • Women’s cottage industries

Economic Challenges

Farmers face challenges like irregular rainfall, low market prices, and high input costs. Access to modern machinery and government schemes remains limited. Seasonal migration is common during drought years.

  • Unpredictable climate conditions
  • Debt due to farming expenses
  • Limited irrigation in some areas

Women & Economy

While most women are homemakers, many are now supporting their families through micro-businesses such as sewing, food preparation, and working in self-help groups (SHGs).

  • Self-help groups (SHGs)
  • Sewing and embroidery
  • Homemade snacks, pickles, and papads
